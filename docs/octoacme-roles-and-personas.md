# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, architecture guidance, and mentorship. They ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with organizational standards. They bridge the gap between high-level strategy and implementation details.

### Responsibilities
- Design technical solutions and system architecture
- Lead technical risk assessments and mitigation strategies
- Mentor development team members and review code quality
- Review and approve technical implementations and design decisions
- Evaluate technology choices and technical debt trade-offs
- Ensure adherence to technical standards and best practices

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Build team technical capability and expertise
- Reduce technical risk and debt
- Enable faster, more reliable delivery

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Works with Project Manager** on feasibility assessment, technical timeline estimates, and resource planning
- **Collaborates with QA Lead** on technical test strategies, automation approaches, and quality metrics
- **Mentors Developers** on architecture patterns, code quality, and technical decisions
- **Reports technical risks to Risk Owner** and participates in risk mitigation planning
- **Partners with Product Manager** on technical feasibility of requirements

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review comments and technical guidance
- Risk assessment documentation and mitigation plans
- Mentoring sessions and technical training

---

## Stakeholder / Product Owner

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Product Owners represent business and customer interests. They define what needs to be built, validate solutions, and ensure the team is working on the highest-value items. They own the product vision and success metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Define requirements and acceptance criteria
- Prioritize backlog items based on business value and impact
- Approve deliverables and validate solutions
- Manage stakeholder expectations and communicate progress
- Participate in decision-making on trade-offs and scope changes
- Track and communicate key success metrics

### Goals
- Maximize business value and customer satisfaction
- Ensure clear alignment between delivery and business objectives
- Reduce scope creep and unplanned work
- Enable rapid feedback and iteration

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Partners with Project Manager** on scope definition, schedule planning, and resource allocation
- **Consults with Technical Lead** on feasibility and technical trade-offs
- **Receives status updates from Project Manager** on progress and risks
- **Collaborates with QA Lead** on acceptance criteria and release readiness
- **Provides guidance to Developers** on requirements and acceptance criteria

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Status reviews and stakeholder briefings
- Requirements documentation and acceptance criteria
- Approval sign-offs and decision documentation

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads own quality strategy, testing approaches, and release readiness. They ensure that deliverables meet quality standards and business requirements. They work across the team to prevent defects and build confidence in releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define QA strategy and comprehensive testing plans
- Ensure quality standards are consistently met across deliverables
- Track, triage, and report defects and quality metrics
- Validate release readiness and coordinate testing activities
- Mentor team members on testing best practices
- Identify and recommend quality improvements

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable solutions
- Reduce defects in production
- Build team quality awareness and skills
- Enable confidence in releases through comprehensive testing

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Works with Technical Lead** on test automation approaches, technical test strategy, and quality metrics
- **Collaborates with Developers** on test coverage, automation, and quality gates
- **Reports quality metrics to Project Manager** and escalates blockers
- **Partners with Stakeholder/Product Owner** on acceptance criteria validation and release approval
- **Coordinates with Communications/Documentation Owner** on test case documentation and quality reporting

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and strategy sessions
- Defect reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Release readiness reports and test summaries
- Quality improvement retrospectives

---

## Communications / Documentation Owner

### Role Summary
Communications and Documentation Owners maintain project transparency and institutional knowledge. They ensure all team members and stakeholders have access to accurate, current information. They facilitate information flow across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain comprehensive project documentation
- Coordinate internal communications and status updates
- Create and update process documentation and templates
- Ensure knowledge transfer and accessibility
- Manage project wikis, runbooks, and reference materials
- Archive and organize project artifacts

### Goals
- Maintain transparent, accessible project information
- Reduce knowledge silos and improve onboarding
- Enable continuous improvement through documented lessons learned
- Support cross-team collaboration through clear communication

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Gathers information from all team members** to ensure comprehensive documentation
- **Reports documentation status to Project Manager** and identifies gaps
- **Works with Technical Lead** on technical documentation and architecture diagrams
- **Collaborates with QA Lead** on test documentation and quality reporting
- **Ensures compliance documentation is current** with Risk Owner

### Typical Communication
- Project status summaries and updates
- Documentation reviews and gap analysis
- Knowledge base and wiki maintenance
- Lessons learned and retrospective documentation

---

## Risk Owner / Compliance Lead

### Role Summary
Risk Owners and Compliance Leads identify, track, and mitigate project risks. They ensure compliance with organizational policies, regulatory requirements, and best practices. They provide early warning of potential issues and coordinate mitigation efforts.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and track project risks across technical, schedule, resource, and business dimensions
- Ensure compliance with organizational standards, regulations, and policies
- Escalate critical issues and coordinate mitigation efforts
- Report on risk and compliance status
- Maintain risk registers and audit trails
- Recommend preventive and corrective actions

### Goals
- Minimize project disruptions and delays
- Ensure organizational and regulatory compliance
- Build organizational resilience through risk management
- Enable proactive problem-solving

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Works with all roles** to identify emerging risks and compliance issues
- **Reports to Project Manager and executive stakeholders** on risk status and escalations
- **Coordinates with Technical Lead** on technical risks and mitigation strategies
- **Partners with Communications Owner** to ensure compliance documentation is maintained
- **Collaborates with QA Lead** on quality and release risks

### Typical Communication
- Risk identification and assessment workshops
- Risk register updates and escalation reports
- Compliance checklists and audit documentation
- Mitigation plan coordination
